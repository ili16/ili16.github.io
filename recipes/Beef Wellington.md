### Classic Beef Wellington Recipe

Beef Wellington is a luxurious dish featuring a tender fillet of beef coated with a rich mushroom duxelles and wrapped in puff pastry. It's perfect for special occasions. Here’s a detailed recipe to impress your guests!

#### Ingredients

**For the Beef:**
- 2 lb (900 g) center-cut beef tenderloin
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp Dijon mustard

**For the Mushroom Duxelles:**
- 1 lb (450 g) mixed mushrooms (cremini, shiitake, or button), finely chopped
- 2 tbsp unsalted butter
- 1 medium shallot, finely chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves (or 1 tsp dried)
- Salt and black pepper to taste

**For Assembly:**
- 1 lb (450 g) prosciutto or Parma ham
- ½ cup (120 ml) pâté (optional: foie gras or mushroom pâté)
- 1 sheet of puff pastry (thawed, if frozen)
- 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
- Flour for dusting

#### Instructions

##### Step 1: Prepare the Beef
1. **Season and Sear:** Season the beef tenderloin generously with salt and pepper. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over high heat. Sear the beef on all sides until browned (about 2-3 minutes per side). Remove from heat and let it cool. Brush with Dijon mustard while still warm. Set aside.

##### Step 2: Make the Mushroom Duxelles
2. **Sauté the Ingredients:** In the same skillet, melt the butter. Add the chopped shallots and garlic, cooking until soft (about 2 minutes).
3. **Cook the Mushrooms:** Add the finely chopped mushrooms and thyme, season with salt and pepper, and cook for about 10-15 minutes, stirring frequently until the moisture has evaporated, and the mixture is thick. Once done, allow the duxelles to cool completely.

##### Step 3: Wrap the Beef
4. **Lay Out the Prosciutto:** On a large sheet of plastic wrap, lay out the slices of prosciutto, slightly overlapping each piece.
5. **Spread the Duxelles:** Evenly spread the cooled mushroom duxelles over the prosciutto.
6. **Add Pâté (Optional):** If using pâté, spread it over the mushroom layer.
7. **Roll the Beef:** Place the cooled beef at one end and, using the plastic wrap, roll it tightly into a log, ensuring the prosciutto and duxelles wrap around the beef. Wrap it tightly in the plastic wrap and refrigerate for about 30 minutes.

##### Step 4: Encase in Puff Pastry
8. **Preheat the Oven:** Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
9. **Roll Out Puff Pastry:** On a lightly floured surface, roll out the puff pastry to about 1/8 inch thick.
10. **Wrap the Beef:** Unwrap the beef from the plastic wrap and place it on the pastry. Wrap the pastry around the beef, sealing the edges. Trim excess pastry and ensure there are no holes. Use the trimmings to decorate the top, if desired.
11. **Egg Wash:** Brush the entire surface of the pastry with the beaten egg for a golden finish.

##### Step 5: Bake
12. **Bake:** Place the wrapped beef seam-side down on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and a meat thermometer inserted into the center reads 130°F (54°C) for medium-rare.

##### Step 6: Serve
13. **Rest and Slice:** Allow the Beef Wellington to rest for 10 minutes before slicing. This helps the juices redistribute.
14. **Plate and Enjoy:** Serve slices with your favorite sides, such as roasted vegetables or a rich red wine reduction.

### Tips:
- For an extra flavor boost, consider adding a splash of brandy or sherry to the mushroom mixture while it cooks.
- You can prepare the mushroom duxelles and wrap the beef up to a day in advance, just make sure to keep it in the refrigerator until ready to bake.

Enjoy your delightful Beef Wellington, a dish fit for royalty!
