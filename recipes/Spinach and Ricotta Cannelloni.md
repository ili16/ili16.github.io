### Spinach and Ricotta Cannelloni

#### Ingredients

**For the Cannelloni:**
- 12-14 cannelloni tubes (store-bought or homemade pasta sheets rolled up)
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 onion, finely chopped
- 10 oz (280 g) fresh spinach, washed and chopped (or 1 package frozen spinach, thawed and drained)
- 15 oz (425 g) ricotta cheese
- 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese, divided
- 1 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
- 1 egg
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Nutmeg, a pinch (optional)

**For the Tomato Sauce:**
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 small onion, chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 can (28 oz or 800 g) crushed tomatoes
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1 teaspoon dried basil
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Fresh basil leaves for garnish (optional)

#### Instructions

1. **Prepare the Tomato Sauce:**
   - In a large saucepan over medium heat, heat the olive oil. Add the chopped onion and cook until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.
   - Add the minced garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
   - Pour in the crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine and let simmer for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld. Adjust seasoning to taste.

2. **Prepare the Spinach and Ricotta Filling:**
   - In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until soft, about 5 minutes.
   - Add the chopped spinach and cook until wilted (if using fresh). If using frozen spinach, work to evaporate any excess moisture. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
   - In a large bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, half of the Parmesan cheese, the egg, and the cooled spinach mixture. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg if desired. Mix until well combined.

3. **Assemble the Cannelloni:**
   - Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
   - Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce on the bottom of a 9x13 inch (or similar) baking dish.
   - Using a teaspoon or piping bag, fill each cannelloni tube with the spinach and ricotta mixture. Place them side by side in the baking dish.
   - Once all the cannelloni are in place, pour the remaining tomato sauce over the top. Spread it evenly to cover the pasta.
   - Sprinkle the remaining Parmesan and the mozzarella cheese over the sauce.

4. **Bake:**
   - Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil (to prevent burning the cheese) and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes. 
   - Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.

5. **Serve:**
   - Allow the cannelloni to cool for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil leaves if desired.

Enjoy your homemade Spinach and Ricotta Cannelloni! This dish pairs beautifully with a side salad and a glass of Italian wine. Buon appetito!
